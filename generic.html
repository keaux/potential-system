Housing Market
Washington County, UT

Housing prices have risen from 57,000 in 1989 to over 650,000.
At the same time, median income has only increased by a much smaller margin of
24,000 to 83,000.

Washington County saw its biggest spike in single-family detached home prices from December 2020 to July 2022. St. George saw median home prices rise from 380,000 to over 600,000; a 58% increase in less than 2 years. This is compared to a nationwide increase of 32%.

The wage gap has widened in recent years, with the lowest-paid workers earning only 66% of what the highest-paid workers make. This increase in income inequality has led to an increase in housing prices out of reach for many people.
